Artwork

Inspired by the sea and the beach, Marina Maiztegui's art is a tribute to the ocean. As a passionate artist, her creative journey revolves around a powerful message: we must appreciate and protect the delicate marine ecosystem, refraining from taking anything from its beaches. Through her work with ceramics, art, and engraving, Marina brings to life an enchanting collection resembling seashell reefs and corals, meticulously crafted to capture the essence of marine treasures. Each piece is a testament to her deep love and respect for nature. Her work includes the "Arrecifes" collection, which features ceramic marine sculptures; the "Oceánica" collection, consisting of artworks with small ceramic pieces on frames; and the "Pacífica" collection, characterized by embossed designs on cotton paper.

Do not take anything from the sea.

Although we might think that the seashells we find on the beach no longer have life and that taking them wouldn't make a difference, the reality is that we are taking away a significant part of the marine ecosystem, which is vital for the life of other species and has a brutal impact.

- Increased beach erosion. The next time you visit that particular beach, there might be less sand.

- Decrease in the abundance of diverse organisms like crabs and other mollusks that depend on the seashells you take.

- Decrease in the abundance of fish. Small fish use shells to hide from other marine creatures that want to eat them.

- Decline in the number of birds. Birds need those shells as construction materials for their nests.

- Decrease in mollusks. New mollusks (clams, oysters, etc.) need calcium carbonate to develop their shells, and they obtain it from the dead shells that erode over time.

- Without the presence of seashells, the water becomes more acidic, putting the life of reefs at risk.

If we add up the above points, the result is a cycle of destruction in the marine and coastal ecosystems.

We can all help, so do not take anything from the sea.
